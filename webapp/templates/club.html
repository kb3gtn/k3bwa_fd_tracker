AN AMATEUR RADIO CLUB IN BOWIE, MD

Who We Are

The Bowie Wireless Association is a community of FCC licensed Amateur Radio Operators dedicated to empowering members with the knowledge and skills needed to excel across HF, VHF, and UHF operations. Experienced members — known as Elmers — mentor newer operators and help grow the hobby throughout the greater Bowie, Maryland area.

Public Service & Emergency Communications

Members actively participate in the Amateur Radio Emergency Service (ARES) and collaborate with the National Weather Service's SkyWarn program in Prince George's County to provide severe weather spotting and warnings. The club also assists local agencies with Incident Command System (ICS) training and supplies communications support for community events.

Activities & Training

The club hosts monthly meetings, ARRL Field Day operations, Parks on the Air (POTA) outings, and hands-on training in digital modes including FT8, DMR, and Winlink. Members also run CW (Morse code) practice sessions and antenna construction workshops throughout the year.
