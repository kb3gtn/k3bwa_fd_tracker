ARRL FIELD DAY
THE LARGEST AMATEUR RADIO EVENT IN NORTH AMERICA

What is Field Day?

ARRL Field Day is an annual emergency preparedness exercise held the last full weekend of June. Amateur radio operators across North America set up temporary stations — often outdoors, off the commercial power grid — to practice deploying emergency communications under realistic conditions.

Scoring

Contacts (QSOs) made during the 24-hour operating period earn points. Phone (voice) contacts are worth 1 point; CW (Morse code) and digital mode contacts are worth 2 points. Bonus multipliers are awarded for working each of the 83 ARRL/RAC sections across the US and Canada. Additional bonus points reward operating on emergency power, satellite contacts, and more.

Operating Categories

Stations compete in classes based on the number of simultaneous transmitters and power source. 1A = 1 transmitter, emergency power. 2A = 2 transmitters, emergency power. Stations on commercial power use the D category. Higher transmitter counts allow more contacts but require more operators and infrastructure.

Why It Matters

When disasters strike and conventional communications collapse, amateur radio operators provide critical support for emergency services. Field Day tests the ability to operate anywhere, anytime, on any available power source — demonstrating that ham radio works when nothing else does.
